# Case Study: Air Quality Risk in Urban India — Hyderabad Focus

## Context
Air pollution is an emerging constraint on urban growth in India.  
For cities like Hyderabad, rising AQI levels have implications beyond health — impacting real estate demand, workforce productivity, and long-term livability.

## Problem
Most city-level AQI analysis is backward-looking and fragmented.  
There is limited forward visibility into how air quality may evolve, making it difficult for stakeholders to plan proactively.

## Approach
An end-to-end analytics pipeline was built to:

- Aggregate and clean historical AQI data across Indian cities  
- Analyze seasonal and long-term pollution trends  
- Forecast future AQI trajectories using time-series modeling (Prophet)  
- Compare risk patterns across cities  

## Key Finding
Hyderabad shows a **consistent upward AQI trend post-2018**, with increasing volatility during peak pollution seasons.

This suggests:
- Pollution is not episodic, but structurally rising  
- Seasonal spikes are becoming more severe  
- Urban expansion may be contributing to long-term degradation  

## Implications
**For Urban Planning**
- Need for stricter emission controls in growth corridors  
- Early investment in air quality monitoring infrastructure  

**For Real Estate**
- Potential downward pressure on premium residential zones over time  
- Opportunity for “clean air” differentiated developments  

**For Policy Makers**
- Shift from reactive measures to predictive interventions  
- Integrate AQI forecasting into city planning frameworks  

## Limitations
- Historical data is limited (majority pre-2020)  
- Forecasts do not yet include external drivers (weather, traffic, industry)  
- Results should be treated as directional, not absolute  

## Next Steps
- Integrate real-time AQI and weather data  
- Build a city-level risk scoring model  
- Extend analysis to Tier-2 cities  
- Deploy as a decision-support dashboard  

## Outcome
This project demonstrates how air quality data can be transformed from static reporting into a **forward-looking risk assessment tool** for urban decision-making.
